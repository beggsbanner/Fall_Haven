# Fall Haven Game Design
## Core Identity

**Fall Haven is a narrative RPG where the player lives through a book that's being written in real time.**

The game is not about playing through a story. It's about **becoming** a character in a story that continues to evolve based on your choices. The narration isn't decoration—it's the engine.

---

## The Core Loop

```
Narration → Choice → Consequence → Story Thread Shift → New Narration
```

Each chapter follows this rhythm:
1. **Scene Narration** (Bard voice)
2. **Character Moment** (emotional beat)
3. **Challenge or Choice** (mechanical decision)
4. **Story Shift** (threads change, tone shifts, new page fragments appear)
5. **Campfire/Rest** (relationship building, reflection)

---

## The Four Acts (Matching the Book)

### Act I: Wonder (Chapters 1–4)
- **Tone**: Curious, warm, slightly dangerous
- **Story Threads**: Curiosity dominant
- **Mechanic**: Discovery. Players are learning Fall Haven.
- **Goal**: Meet the Fall Haven 9, accept the quest for the scrolls

### Act II: The Road (Chapters 5–14)
- **Tone**: Travel, humor, growing bonds, strange encounters
- **Story Threads**: Humor + Bond growing
- **Mechanic**: Journey mechanics. Campfires are story-changing moments.
- **Goal**: Reach Booger Hollow. Establish party dynamics.

### Act III: Home & Preparation (Chapters 15–24)
- **Tone**: Community, mystery, gathering power
- **Story Threads**: Bond strong, Fear rising
- **Mechanic**: Hub exploration + Rumor system. Page fragments unlock secrets.
- **Goal**: Prepare for war. Uncover the truth of the goblins and Tetrahedron.

### Act IV: War & Aftermath (Chapters 25–36 + Epilogue)
- **Tone**: Desperate, chaotic, loss, mystery
- **Story Threads**: Fear dominant, all threads in tension
- **Mechanic**: Large-scale battles, emotional moments, ambiguity.
- **Goal**: Survive. Discover what's truly at stake. Set up the next journey.

## Story Thread Mechanics

### The Four Threads
- **Curiosity**: Unlocks dialogue, reveals secrets, page fragments
- **Humor**: Defuses tension, builds relationships, keeps morale up
- **Fear**: Creates pressure, darkens tone, reveals hidden dangers
- **Bond**: Unlocks cooperative actions, makes allies stronger, protects party

### How Threads Work in Gameplay

**During a scene:**
- A skill check uses the relevant thread
- Dad's humor might diffuse a goblin encounter (Humor)
- Goldilocks' curiosity might uncover a secret shortcut (Curiosity)
- Mrs. Bigfoot's strength matters when Fear is high (Bond reduces Fear)

**Between scenes:**
- Campfire conversations shift the thread balance
- A story beat might reduce Humor, increase Fear
- Page fragments found change how scenes play out retroactively

### Page Fragments

**What they are**: Broken pieces of the story that can be restored
- Found by exploring and succeeding at challenges
- Can be spent to:
  - Rewrite a failed scene
  - Unlock hidden dialogue
  - Restore a fallen ally (temporarily)
  - Change the tone of the next chapter

**Mechanic**: Players earn 1–2 per chapter. Spending one resets that chapter's outcome.

---

## Combat System (Sparse but Meaningful)

Combat is **not** the focus. It happens, but:

1. **Most conflicts resolve through skill checks** tied to story threads
   - Sneak past goblins (Curiosity/Sneak)
   - Charm a guard (Humor/Charm)
   - Stand firm under pressure (Bond/Courage)

2. **When combat happens**, it's narrated as a **sequence of choices**, not turn-based
   - Each choice advances the scene
   - Failure doesn't mean death; it means consequence
   - Multiple solutions exist (fight, flee, negotiate)

3. **The final battle** (Chapter 36) is cinematic and scripted
   - Player actions matter but the outcome is driven by story
   - The "loss" of Goldilocks' family is a scripted moment
   - Sets up the mystery for what comes next

---

## Skill System

### Core Skills (tied to story threads)
- **Sneak** (Curiosity) — Move unseen, find secrets
- **Charm** (Humor) — Persuade, defuse, build alliances
- **Courage** (Bond) — Stand firm, protect others, resist fear
- **Knowledge** (Curiosity) — Understand lore, decode scrolls
- **Performance** (Humor) — Dad's lute, storytelling, morale
- **Survival** (Curiosity/Fear) — Navigate danger, find resources

### How They Matter
- Each skill check adds to a thread
- Failure doesn't end the scene—it shifts the tone
- Multiple skills can solve the same problem
- Using the "wrong" skill creates interesting complications

## The Narration System

### The Bard
- A voice (or text) that narrates key moments
- Can be funny, serious, or ominous
- Reflects the current story tone:
  - **Curious tone**: Wonder, discovery, mystery
  - **Playful tone**: Humor, absurdity, lightness
  - **Tense tone**: Danger, pressure, darkness
  - **Fractured tone**: Confusion, loss, ambiguity

### Narration Triggers
- Chapter introductions
- Major story beats
- Campfire moments
- Combat sequences
- Discoveries

---

## The Campfire System

**Campfires are gameplay.**

At each campfire, the player:
1. **Chooses who to talk to**
   - Conversations reveal character backstory
   - Some conversations shift story threads
   - Relationship points accumulate (but aren't shown as numbers)

2. **Can spend page fragments**
   - Rewrite a recent scene
   - Unlock a hidden memory
   - Restore someone's morale

3. **Gets narration reflecting the journey**
   - Bard recounts what's happened
   - Characters share fears and hopes
   - The tone of the next chapter is set

**Example Campfire (Chapter 5):**
- Goldilocks and Dad tell each other bedtime stories
- Player chooses which story to tell
- Choice affects how Goldilocks will approach the next challenge
- Ticklesticks' song plays, shifting the tone

---

## The Chapter Structure

Each chapter is roughly:
- **10–15 min of narrated story**
- **1–2 skill challenges or choices**
- **Campfire moment** (3–5 min)
- **Story consequence** (thread shifts, new information)

---

## How the Book Becomes the Game

### Pre-Game: The Story is Fixed
- Chapters 1–36 follow the book
- Campfire choices don't "branch" into new chapters
- The story moves forward no matter what

### Within Chapters: Real Agency
- Skill checks change how scenes play out
- Page fragments let players rewrite outcomes
- Tone shifts based on thread balance
- Narration acknowledges your choices

### The Epilogue and Beyond
- Chapter 36 ends on ambiguity (Goldilocks vanishes)
- The game continues into the epilogue
- Ticklesticks' mysterious mission becomes the new story
- **This is where player choice actually shapes what comes next**

---

## Unique Mechanics from the Book

### The Tetrahedron Balance
- 4 faces in story: Imagination, Harmony, Courage, Wisdom
- 2 hidden faces: Chaos, Madness
- The game doesn't explain this directly—players discover it
- The balance affects what's possible in the world

### The Automaton Perspective (Morvin)
- One playable character is mechanical
- His scenes have different rules (snot is a problem, steam bank management)
- His growth arc (from desk clerk to hero) mirrors the player's arc

### The Family Dynamic
- Dad's humor is mechanically important
- Mom's strength isn't just flavor
- Siblings each have unique skills
- Relationships matter to party performance

### The Cryptid Army
- Not just NPCs; they're allies with their own arcs
- Mrs. Bigfoot's leadership changes what's possible
- Tom's tracking reveals hidden paths
- Ticklesticks' final departure is a real loss

---

## Tone Anchors

**This game should feel like:**
- A folk tale being told aloud
- An 80s fantasy adventure (with self-aware humor)
- A family story with real stakes
- A mystery that deepens with each chapter
- A game where silly moments and tragic moments coexist

**It should NOT feel like:**
- A traditional RPG with levels and loot
- A branching visual novel
- A puzzle box to "solve"
- A power fantasy (Ticklesticks can still lose)

---

## Success Metrics

The game succeeds if players feel:
1. **"I'm living this story, not reading it"**
2. **"My choices mattered, even though the ending was set"**
3. **"The tone matched the narration"**
4. **"The characters felt real and earned my care"**
5. **"I want to know what happens next"**
